Physics

Physics is the study of everything. It is the search for the fundamental rules that govern the universe, from the subatomic jitter of a quark to the slow, gravitational waltz of colliding galaxies.

At its heart, physics is not about memorizing formulas. It is about understanding the Symmetry and Logic of the universe. It is the realization that the same principles that cause a coffee cup to cool also dictate the eventual fate of all stars.

Let's strip away the complexity and look at reality from absolute first principles.

THERMODYNAMICS

1. The Architecture of Chaos (Entropy)

Why does time only move in one direction? Why do we never see a broken egg spontaneously reassemble itself? The answer lies in Entropy.

Entropy is often described as "disorder," but it is more accurately the number of ways a system can be arranged. There are billions of ways for gas molecules to be scattered randomly, but only one way for them all to be huddled in a single corner. Nature naturally drifts toward the most probable state: chaos.

Observe how the particles spread evenly over time. This uniform distribution is the state of maximum entropy.

2. The Wave-Particle Duality

On the smallest scales, the universe stops acting like a machine and starts acting like a dream. Particles like electrons do not have a single, definite location. Instead, they exist as a Wave of Probability.

When we aren't looking, an electron is "everywhere" at once. Only when we measure it does the wave "collapse" into a single point. This is the heart of the Double-Slit experiment: things can be both a localized particle and a spreading wave simultaneously.

3. The Fabric of Reality (Relativity)

Space and time are not separate. They are woven together into a four-dimensional fabric called Space-time. Mass doesn't just "pull" on things; it actually warps the geometry of this fabric.

Imagine placing a bowling ball on a trampoline. The fabric dips. If you roll a marble nearby, it will curve toward the ball—not because of an invisible force, but because it is following the curvature of the surface. This curvature is what we feel as Gravity.
